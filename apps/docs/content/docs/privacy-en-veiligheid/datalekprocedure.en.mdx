## What is a data breach?

A data breach is a security incident where personal data becomes unintentionally accessible, is lost, or is altered. Examples include:

- Unauthorised access to the database
- A bug that exposes user data
- Loss of encryption keys
- A compromised admin account

<Callout type="info">
  Because your chat messages are end-to-end encrypted, their content cannot be read even in the
  event of a data breach.
</Callout>

## What do we do in case of a data breach?

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Detection & assessment

    We continuously monitor our systems. As soon as we detect a possible breach, we assess within 24 hours:
    - What data was affected?
    - How many users are involved?
    - Has the breach been contained?

  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Containment

    We take immediate action to prevent further damage: revoking credentials, deploying security patches, and closing the breach.

  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Notification to the Dutch Data Protection Authority

    If the breach poses a risk to your rights, we report it within 72 hours to the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (Dutch DPA), as required by the GDPR.

  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Notification to you

    If the breach poses a high risk, we inform you personally via email and/or an in-app notification. We tell you:
    - What happened
    - What data was involved
    - What we've done
    - What you can do

  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Evaluation

    Afterwards, we conduct an analysis to prevent recurrence and adjust our security measures where needed.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## When you don't need to worry

Not every security incident is cause for concern. You have less reason to worry if:

- The affected data was encrypted and the keys were not leaked
- The incident was immediately contained and no data was exposed
- Only anonymised data was involved

## Reporting a suspected breach

Think something is wrong with your data, or noticed suspicious activity? Contact us immediately:

- **Email**: privacy@openhospi.nl
- **In the app**: Use the report function

<Callout type="warn">
  We take every report seriously and respond as quickly as possible. Don't hesitate to reach out,
  even if you're not sure whether it's a breach.
</Callout>
